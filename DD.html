Round 1: BRAIN TEASER

This round consists of Quantity aptitude, Reasoning (logical ability) and Verbal ability (English), which usually consists of 25 MCQs. These tests are designed to evaluate a candidate’s ability to understand complex problems. They involve logical thinking, critical analysis, and problem-solving skills that are necessary to navigate through placements drives and competitive examinations.

Round 2: TECH-CODE CHALLENGE

The second round of this event is the technical round, which usually consists of 10 MCQs related to programming. The questions are designed to test your knowledge of programming concepts and your ability to apply them to solve problems. Dive deep into the technical realm with programming questions. Showcase your coding prowess and problem-solving abilities to impress potential employers.

Round 3: BRAINSTROMING SESSION

The third round of a typical placement drive is the group discussion round, which usually consists of 4 batches, each consisting of 10 members. The purpose of this round is to evaluate a candidate’s communication skills, leadership qualities, and ability to work in a team. During the discussion, candidates are given a topic to discuss, and they are evaluated based on their ability to present their ideas effectively, listen to others, and work collaboratively.

Round 4: INTERVIEW

For the final showdown, face real-world scenarios and answer questions on-thespot. Demonstrate your communication skills and industry knowledge for a chance at placement success. During the interview, candidates are asked questions about their background, experience, and interests.
